Tennis: From Scottish Roots to Global Equality

Tennis has a very long and interesting history in Scotland. For example, the Great Hall at Stirling Castle and Falkland Palace had tennis courts back in the 16th century. In the nineteenth century, tennis was a fairly exclusive sport, with high level participation being mainly by the upper classes. Scotland produced two Wimbledon men's champions in this period, Herbert Lawford and Harold Mahony.

Harold Mahony - Wimbledon Champion

There are four major tournaments that every professional player dreams of winning:

  • Australian Open
  • Roland Garros
  • Wimbledon
  • US Open

Rod Laver Arena, Melbourne

Billie Jean King is a tennis legend who fought for equality. In 1973, she won the famous "Battle of the Sexes" match against Bobby Riggs. Her work helped ensure that women and men receive equal prize money at all Grand Slam tournaments today.
